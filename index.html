1. The "Underinsured" Paradox

Insurance status remains the strongest predictor of access to care.

The data uncovers a "hidden" crisis among the working population that rivals the challenges faced by the uninsured.

The Uninsured Crisis

61.1% report cost as a major barrier. Effectively excluded from the dental system (47.2%) and struggling significantly with primary care (40.3%).

The "Working Poor" & Appointments

Patients with Employer-Sponsored Insurance report the highest rate (24.7%) of difficulty securing a General Doctor appointment—double that of Medicaid/Medicare.

The Protective Role of Medicaid

Medicaid respondents reported the lowest overall cost barriers (26.4%), indicating the safety net effectively protects enrollees from out-of-pocket costs.

2. A Tale of Three Valleys

The data reveals that "access" means fundamentally different things depending on where a patient lives.

UAV Region: High Financial Strain

Upper Arkansas Valley (UAV)

Financial Strain & Specialist Crisis

The epicenter of cost barriers (37.4%). Residents face the highest hurdles accessing Specialist Care (18.0% cost barrier).

Mental Health Stigma

Highest rate of Stigma (5.2%) as a barrier—double other regions—and highest cost barriers for mental health (9.5%).

San Luis Valley (SLV)

Capacity Bottlenecks

Operational capacity is the primary challenge. Highest complaints regarding "Wait time" (19.3%) and difficulty finding services (20.6%).

Primary Care Access

Hardest region to see a General Doctor—18.4% unable to get an appointment when needed.

Wait times peak in SLV

LAV Region: Dental Access vs Cost

Lower Arkansas Valley (LAV)

The Physical Barrier

While cost and appointments are less of a barrier here (good dental access), 13.1% cite Transportation as a major hurdle. Getting to the clinic is the hardest part.

3. Communication & Outreach

Personal Networks

"Close friends or family" is the dominant information source (~56%). Word-of-mouth beats billboards.

Digital Trends

Social media usage for health info is highest in the LAV (31.1%).

Medicaid Outreach

Medicaid recipients are uniquely responsive to Local Public Health Agencies (42.9%).

Strategic Path Forward

  • Expand Dental Capacity: Specifically target Medicare and Uninsured populations.
  • Fix SLV Efficiency: Address operational bottlenecks to reduce wait times.
  • Solve "Last Mile" in LAV: Implement transportation support or mobile units.
  • Support Working Poor: Extended hours for those with Employer-Sponsored Insurance.
